Home Depot Product Search Relevance Model¶

Shoppers rely on Home Depots product authority to find and buy the latest products and to get timely solutions to their home improvement needs. From installing a new ceiling fan to remodeling an entire kitchen, with the click of a mouse or tap of the screen, customers expect the correct results to their queries quickly. Speed, accuracy and delivering a frictionless customer experience are essential.

In this project, I will help improve their customers' shopping experience by developing a model that can predict the relevance of search results relatively well.

Search relevancy is an implicit measure Home Depot uses to gauge how quickly they can get customers to the right products. Currently, human raters evaluate the impact of potential changes to their search algorithms, which is a slow and subjective process. By removing or minimizing human input in search relevance evaluation, Home Depot hopes to increase the number of iterations their team can perform on the current search algorithms.

The function string_stemmer below helps reduce a word to its simplest form, meaning that it removes any tense, plural form, essentially all morphological affixes from words, leaving only the word stem. By having the simplest word, I can increase the frequency of a single word in the description (now indistinguishable without the affixes) and improve the modeling accuracy.

In [111]:
def string_stemmer(string):
    return ' '.join([stemmer.stem(word) for word in string.lower().split()])

The function common_word below searches for the number of common words in string1 from string2. It will help me determine the frequency of words.

In [112]:
def common_word(string1, string2):
    return sum([int(string2.find(word)>=0) for word in string1.split()])
